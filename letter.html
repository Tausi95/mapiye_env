Dear Honourable Visa Officer,

I, Costantine Mapiye, a Malawian national holding permanent residency in South Africa, respectfully submit this letter in support of my application for a B1/B2 non-immigrant visa to the United States of America. I am currently employed as the Business and Marketing Manager at New Balance Athletics, Cape Town, where I have served for over six years in progressive leadership roles within the brand's South African retail operations.

The primary purpose of my intended visit to the United States is professional development, specifically to:

  • Attend brand strategy sessions at New Balance North America headquarters in Boston, Massachusetts, to align global marketing direction with our Cape Town operations.
  • Participate in the NB Retail Manager's Immersion Programme, a structured learning initiative designed for senior managers across the global New Balance retail network.
  • Conduct a retail study tour of flagship New Balance stores in New York City, Boston, and Los Angeles to benchmark store experience, layout, and consumer engagement best practices for implementation at our Cape Town locations.
  • Meet with prospective brand partners and sports marketing stakeholders based in the United States.

My visit is strictly professional and time-bound. I have confirmed accommodation at pre-arranged business hotels for the duration of my stay (full itinerary enclosed). I will be in the United States for a period of 14 days, departing from Cape Town International Airport on 15 May 2026 and returning no later than 29 May 2026.

I have strong and compelling ties to South Africa that assure my return:

  • I hold a permanent resident status in South Africa and am legally bound to maintain my residency obligations.
  • I am currently in an active employment contract with New Balance Cape Town and am expected back at work upon my return.
  • I have financial commitments in Cape Town including property lease obligations and dependants.
  • I actively manage ongoing marketing campaigns and business partnerships that require my physical presence in Cape Town.

I am fully able to support myself financially throughout my stay in the United States. I have a valid South African bank account with sufficient funds, and all professional engagements are partially sponsored by New Balance South Africa (sponsor letter enclosed separately).

I attach the following supporting documents to this application:

  • Certified copy of valid passport (PP No. [PASSPORT NO.])
  • Proof of South African Permanent Residence Permit
  • Letter of employment from New Balance Cape Town
  • Company sponsorship letter from New Balance South Africa
  • Confirmed flight and accommodation bookings
  • Detailed travel itinerary (enclosed)
  • Bank statements — last 3 months
  • South African Income Tax registration confirmation
  • Proof of residential address in Cape Town

I am confident that I meet all the requirements for the issuance of a B1/B2 visa and that my visit will be fully consistent with the conditions and terms of a non-immigrant visa. I have never violated any visa terms in any country previously visited and have a clean travel record. I sincerely request your favourable consideration of this application and remain available for any further information or an interview at your earliest convenience.

I thank you for your time and kind consideration.
